Ratan Naval Tata (28 December 1937 â€“ 9 October 2024) was an Indian industrialist and philanthropist. He served as the chairman of Tata Group and Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012 and he held the position of interim chairman from October 2016 to February 2017. In 2000, he received the Padma Bhushan, the third highest civilian honor in India, followed by the Padma Vibhushan, the country's second highest civilian honor, in 2008.

Ratan Tata was the son of Naval Tata, who was adopted by Ratanji Tata, son of Jamshedji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group. He graduated from Cornell University College of Architecture with a bachelor's degree in architecture. He had also attended the Harvard Business School (HBS) Advanced Management program in 1975. He joined the Tata Group in 1962, starting on the shop floor of Tata Steel. He later succeeded J. R. D. Tata as chairman of Tata Sons upon the latter's retirement in 1991. During his tenure, the Tata Group acquired Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus, in an attempt to turn Tata from a largely India-centric group into a global business.

Throughout his life, Tata invested in over 40 start-ups, primarily in a personal capacity, with additional investments through his firm, RNT Capital Advisors.

Biographies

  • Birth and Early Life: Born on 28 December 1937 in Bombay, British India (now Mumbai), Ratan Naval Tata was the son of Naval Tata and Sooni Commissariat. His parents separated when he was 10 years old, and he was subsequently adopted by his grandmother, Navajbai Tata, through the J. N. Petit Parsi Orphanage.
  • Education: Attended Campion School, Cathedral and John Connon School, Bishop Cotton School, and Riverdale Country School in New York City. Graduated from Cornell University College of Architecture with a bachelor's degree in architecture and attended the Harvard Business School's Advanced Management program in 1975.
  • Family Background: Part of the Tata family, a prominent Parsi Zoroastrian business dynasty. His grandfather, Hormusji Tata, was a member of the Tata family by blood, while his father, Naval Tata, was adopted by Ratanji Tata, son of Jamshedji Tata, the founder of the Tata Group.
  • Career Joined the Tata Group in 1962, starting on the shop floor of Tata Steel. Served as the chairman of Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012 and held the position of interim chairman from October 2016 to February 2017. He succeeded J. R. D. Tata as chairman of Tata Sons in 1991.
  • Honors and Awards: Received numerous accolades, including the Padma Bhushan (2000), Padma Vibhushan (2008), Order of Australia (2023), and Honorary Knight Grand Cross of the Order of the British Empire (2014).
  • Philanthropy and Leadership: Known for his philanthropic activities and personal ethics, he received awards such as the Responsible Capitalism Award (2006), Carnegie Medal of Philanthropy (2007), and Business for Peace Award (2010).
  • Death: Passed away on 9 October 2024 at the age of 86 due to age-related health issues, admitted to Mumbai's Breach Candy Hospital and later moved to the ICU.
